</style><div style="padding-bottom:12px;padding-left:10px;padding-right:10px;padding-top:12px;"><p style="color:#000000;color:var(--wt-text-on-background-color) !important;font-family:'Helvetica',Arial,sans-serif;font-size:19px;line-height:1.5;text-align:left;"> It wasn’t shocking when San Francisco’s moderate Democratic mayor said she’d join Republicans calling for <a class="link" href="https://www.mercurynews.com/2024/02/08/bay-area-mayors-join-move-to-walk-back-prop-47-and-crack-down-on-thefts-and-drugs/?utm_source=morningtrojan.com&utm_medium=referral&utm_campaign=are-california-democrats-shifting-right" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer nofollow" style="-webkit-text-decoration:underline #ac2124;color:#ac2124;text-decoration:underline #ac2124;word-break:break-word;;">rollbacks to Proposition 47</a>, a controversial ballot measure that eased penalties for some low-level crimes.  </p></div><style>
  p span[style*="font-size"] { line-height: 1.6; }
</style><div style="padding-bottom:12px;padding-left:10px;padding-right:10px;padding-top:12px;"><p style="color:#000000;color:var(--wt-text-on-background-color) !important;font-family:'Helvetica',Arial,sans-serif;font-size:19px;line-height:1.5;text-align:left;"> It also wasn’t much of a surprise when Democratic lawmakers joined Republicans calling for <a class="link" href="https://calmatters.org/housing/homelessness/2024/02/homeless-encampments-california-ban/?utm_source=morningtrojan.com&utm_medium=referral&utm_campaign=are-california-democrats-shifting-right" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer nofollow" style="-webkit-text-decoration:underline #ac2124;color:#ac2124;text-decoration:underline #ac2124;word-break:break-word;;">significant restrictions</a> on homeless encampments across the state earlier this month. </p></div><style>
  p span[style*="font-size"] { line-height: 1.6; }
</style><div style="padding-bottom:12px;padding-left:10px;padding-right:10px;padding-top:12px;"><p style="color:#000000;color:var(--wt-text-on-background-color) !important;font-family:'Helvetica',Arial,sans-serif;font-size:19px;line-height:1.5;text-align:left;"> But what those two moves signaled might be something larger: Could California Democrats maybe — just maybe — be shifting to the right? </p></div><div id="a-course-correction" style="padding-bottom:1px;padding-left:10px;padding-right:10px;padding-top:6px;"><h1 style="color:#000000;font-family:'Helvetica',Arial,sans-serif;font-size:32px;font-weight:Bold;line-height:1.75;margin:0;text-align:left;">A course correction</h1></div><style>
  p span[style*="font-size"] { line-height: 1.6; }
</style><div style="padding-bottom:12px;padding-left:10px;padding-right:10px;padding-top:12px;"><p style="color:#000000;color:var(--wt-text-on-background-color) !important;font-family:'Helvetica',Arial,sans-serif;font-size:19px;line-height:1.5;text-align:left;"> Yes, said Dan Schnur, who teaches politics at USC, UC Berkeley and Pepperdine. There has been a shift. </p></div><style>
  p span[style*="font-size"] { line-height: 1.6; }
</style><div style="padding-bottom:12px;padding-left:10px;padding-right:10px;padding-top:12px;"><p style="color:#000000;color:var(--wt-text-on-background-color) !important;font-family:'Helvetica',Arial,sans-serif;font-size:19px;line-height:1.5;text-align:left;"> “What we’re seeing now is not necessarily a return to the 1990s, but perhaps just back to the 20-teens,” Schnur said. </p></div><style>
  p span[style*="font-size"] { line-height: 1.6; }
</style><div style="padding-bottom:12px;padding-left:10px;padding-right:10px;padding-top:12px;"><p style="color:#000000;color:var(--wt-text-on-background-color) !important;font-family:'Helvetica',Arial,sans-serif;font-size:19px;line-height:1.5;text-align:left;"> In the summer of 2020, the high-profile killings of George Floyd and Breonna Taylor turbocharged what had been a steady leftward shift in the national conversation about criminal justice over the past few decades.  </p></div><style>
  p span[style*="font-size"] { line-height: 1.6; }
</style><div style="padding-bottom:12px;padding-left:10px;padding-right:10px;padding-top:12px;"><p style="color:#000000;color:var(--wt-text-on-background-color) !important;font-family:'Helvetica',Arial,sans-serif;font-size:19px;line-height:1.5;text-align:left;"> But that might’ve pushed Democrats too far left, Schnur said. Today’s policies are adjusting for that. </p></div><style>
  p span[style*="font-size"] { line-height: 1.6; }
</style><div style="padding-bottom:12px;padding-left:10px;padding-right:10px;padding-top:12px;"><p style="color:#000000;color:var(--wt-text-on-background-color) !important;font-family:'Helvetica',Arial,sans-serif;font-size:19px;line-height:1.5;text-align:left;"> “‘Course correction’ is probably the right word to use,” said Bill Lockyer, the former California attorney general. “But I would say it’s a four-degree shift, not an 80-degree shift.” </p></div><style>
  p span[style*="font-size"] { line-height: 1.6; }
</style><div style="padding-bottom:12px;padding-left:10px;padding-right:10px;padding-top:12px;"><p style="color:#000000;color:var(--wt-text-on-background-color) !important;font-family:'Helvetica',Arial,sans-serif;font-size:19px;line-height:1.5;text-align:left;"> Lawmakers don’t always get everything right on the first try, and Lockyer said he sees things like proposed rollbacks to Prop. 47 — the 2014 crime measure that the moderate mayors of San Francisco and San Jose want to amend — as fine-tuning of legislation. </p></div><style>
  p span[style*="font-size"] { line-height: 1.6; }
</style><div style="padding-bottom:12px;padding-left:10px;padding-right:10px;padding-top:12px;"><p style="color:#000000;color:var(--wt-text-on-background-color) !important;font-family:'Helvetica',Arial,sans-serif;font-size:19px;line-height:1.5;text-align:left;"> Note that although the decade-old measure isn’t a product of the Black Lives Matter movement, it <i>is </i>an obvious target for any lawmaker looking to crack down on crime: Republicans have already mounted a perennial effort to roll it back.  </p></div><style>
  p span[style*="font-size"] { line-height: 1.6; }
</style><div style="padding-bottom:12px;padding-left:10px;padding-right:10px;padding-top:12px;"><p style="color:#000000;color:var(--wt-text-on-background-color) !important;font-family:'Helvetica',Arial,sans-serif;font-size:19px;line-height:1.5;text-align:left;"> But today’s rightward tilt could have as much to do with the rhetoric of the Black Lives Matter movement as any particular policy. </p></div><style>
  p span[style*="font-size"] { line-height: 1.6; }
</style><div style="padding-bottom:12px;padding-left:10px;padding-right:10px;padding-top:12px;"><p style="color:#000000;color:var(--wt-text-on-background-color) !important;font-family:'Helvetica',Arial,sans-serif;font-size:19px;line-height:1.5;text-align:left;"> “I think there will be studies in the future about the degree to which the phrase ‘defund the police’ contributed to this countershift,” Schnur said. </p></div><style>
  p span[style*="font-size"] { line-height: 1.6; }
</style><div style="padding-bottom:12px;padding-left:10px;padding-right:10px;padding-top:12px;"><p style="color:#000000;color:var(--wt-text-on-background-color) !important;font-family:'Helvetica',Arial,sans-serif;font-size:19px;line-height:1.5;text-align:left;"> Defunding the police, a rallying cry during 2020’s massive protests, <a class="link" href="https://www.pewresearch.org/short-reads/2021/10/26/growing-share-of-americans-say-they-want-more-spending-on-police-in-their-area/?utm_source=morningtrojan.com&utm_medium=referral&utm_campaign=are-california-democrats-shifting-right" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer nofollow" style="-webkit-text-decoration:underline #ac2124;color:#ac2124;text-decoration:underline #ac2124;word-break:break-word;;">was never</a> a popular front. It isn’t particularly hard to see how a moderate frustrated with crime today might conflate those far-left cries with criminal justice reform in general. </p></div><style>
  p span[style*="font-size"] { line-height: 1.6; }
</style><div style="padding-bottom:12px;padding-left:10px;padding-right:10px;padding-top:12px;"><p style="color:#000000;color:var(--wt-text-on-background-color) !important;font-family:'Helvetica',Arial,sans-serif;font-size:19px;line-height:1.5;text-align:left;"> “‘Defund the police’ was one of the dumbest political slogans ever invented,” Lockyer said. “It wasn’t going to happen — it was kind of a phony issue. And it was a way to label reformers badly and say ‘They just don’t want police, they want anarchy.’” </p></div><div id="perception-over-numbers" style="padding-bottom:1px;padding-left:10px;padding-right:10px;padding-top:6px;"><h1 style="color:#000000;font-family:'Helvetica',Arial,sans-serif;font-size:32px;font-weight:Bold;line-height:1.75;margin:0;text-align:left;">Perception over numbers</h1></div><style>
  p span[style*="font-size"] { line-height: 1.6; }
</style><div style="padding-bottom:12px;padding-left:10px;padding-right:10px;padding-top:12px;"><p style="color:#000000;color:var(--wt-text-on-background-color) !important;font-family:'Helvetica',Arial,sans-serif;font-size:19px;line-height:1.5;text-align:left;"> Prop. 47 was intended to reduce overcrowding in state prisons by reclassifying some non-violent property and drug possession crimes as misdemeanors. On that front, <a class="link" href="https://www.ppic.org/publication/how-has-proposition-47-affected-californias-jail-population/?utm_source=morningtrojan.com&utm_medium=referral&utm_campaign=are-california-democrats-shifting-right" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer nofollow" style="-webkit-text-decoration:underline #ac2124;color:#ac2124;text-decoration:underline #ac2124;word-break:break-word;;">it worked</a>, and it <a class="link" href="https://www.ppic.org/publication/the-impact-of-proposition-47-on-crime-and-recidivism/?utm_source=morningtrojan.com&utm_medium=referral&utm_campaign=are-california-democrats-shifting-right" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer nofollow" style="-webkit-text-decoration:underline #ac2124;color:#ac2124;text-decoration:underline #ac2124;word-break:break-word;;">likely didn’t contribute</a> to any rise in crime while at it. </p></div><style>
  p span[style*="font-size"] { line-height: 1.6; }
</style><div style="padding-bottom:12px;padding-left:10px;padding-right:10px;padding-top:12px;"><p style="color:#000000;color:var(--wt-text-on-background-color) !important;font-family:'Helvetica',Arial,sans-serif;font-size:19px;line-height:1.5;text-align:left;"> Crime statistics are often mixed and sometimes <a class="link" href="https://www.sfchronicle.com/sf/article/shoplifting-data-Target-Walgreens-16647769.php?utm_source=morningtrojan.com&utm_medium=referral&utm_campaign=are-california-democrats-shifting-right" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer nofollow" style="-webkit-text-decoration:underline #ac2124;color:#ac2124;text-decoration:underline #ac2124;word-break:break-word;;">misleading</a>, and it’s not always clear what drives the ebbs and flows. But we do know that crime spiked during the pandemic then <a class="link" href="https://www.ppic.org/blog/two-years-of-wide-variation-in-crime-trends/?utm_source=morningtrojan.com&utm_medium=referral&utm_campaign=are-california-democrats-shifting-right" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer nofollow" style="-webkit-text-decoration:underline #ac2124;color:#ac2124;text-decoration:underline #ac2124;word-break:break-word;;">leveled off</a>, and San Francisco and Los Angeles have <a class="link" href="https://www.latimes.com/california/story/2023-06-01/crime-is-down-in-la-as-city-plans-to-expand-lapd?utm_source=morningtrojan.com&utm_medium=referral&utm_campaign=are-california-democrats-shifting-right" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer nofollow" style="-webkit-text-decoration:underline #ac2124;color:#ac2124;text-decoration:underline #ac2124;word-break:break-word;;">generally gotten safer</a> over the last few years. Notably, though, retail theft <a class="link" href="https://xtown.la/2024/02/12/shoplifting-in-city-of-los-angeles-soars-81-in-2023/?utm_source=morningtrojan.com&utm_medium=referral&utm_campaign=are-california-democrats-shifting-right" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer nofollow" style="-webkit-text-decoration:underline #ac2124;color:#ac2124;text-decoration:underline #ac2124;word-break:break-word;;">went up</a> in 2023, and the state has found itself in the throes of a <a class="link" href="https://www.sfchronicle.com/projects/san-francisco-drug-overdose-deaths/?utm_source=morningtrojan.com&utm_medium=referral&utm_campaign=are-california-democrats-shifting-right" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer nofollow" style="-webkit-text-decoration:underline #ac2124;color:#ac2124;text-decoration:underline #ac2124;word-break:break-word;;">deadly opioid epidemic</a>.  </p></div><style>
  p span[style*="font-size"] { line-height: 1.6; }
</style><div style="padding-bottom:12px;padding-left:10px;padding-right:10px;padding-top:12px;"><p style="color:#000000;color:var(--wt-text-on-background-color) !important;font-family:'Helvetica',Arial,sans-serif;font-size:19px;line-height:1.5;text-align:left;"> But how voters feel about crime often has <a class="link" href="https://calmatters.org/commentary/2023/09/los-angeles-crime/?utm_source=morningtrojan.com&utm_medium=referral&utm_campaign=are-california-democrats-shifting-right" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer nofollow" style="-webkit-text-decoration:underline #ac2124;color:#ac2124;text-decoration:underline #ac2124;word-break:break-word;;">nothing to do</a> with the actual numbers. </p></div><style>
  p span[style*="font-size"] { line-height: 1.6; }
</style><div style="padding-bottom:12px;padding-left:10px;padding-right:10px;padding-top:12px;"><p style="color:#000000;color:var(--wt-text-on-background-color) !important;font-family:'Helvetica',Arial,sans-serif;font-size:19px;line-height:1.5;text-align:left;"> “Voters react very strongly to particular instances,” Schnur said. “The statistics are important to them, but the memory of a specific crime resonates even more deeply.” </p></div><style>
  p span[style*="font-size"] { line-height: 1.6; }
</style><div style="padding-bottom:12px;padding-left:10px;padding-right:10px;padding-top:12px;"><p style="color:#000000;color:var(--wt-text-on-background-color) !important;font-family:'Helvetica',Arial,sans-serif;font-size:19px;line-height:1.5;text-align:left;"> So when footage of smash-and-grab robberies at California retail stores aired day after day on TV last year, voters were watching. Same for when a tech executive <a class="link" href="https://sfstandard.com/2023/07/31/san-francisco-nima-momeni-bob-lee-trial/?utm_source=morningtrojan.com&utm_medium=referral&utm_campaign=are-california-democrats-shifting-right" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer nofollow" style="-webkit-text-decoration:underline #ac2124;color:#ac2124;text-decoration:underline #ac2124;word-break:break-word;;">was murdered</a> on the street in San Francisco.  </p></div><style>
  p span[style*="font-size"] { line-height: 1.6; }
</style><div style="padding-bottom:12px;padding-left:10px;padding-right:10px;padding-top:12px;"><p style="color:#000000;color:var(--wt-text-on-background-color) !important;font-family:'Helvetica',Arial,sans-serif;font-size:19px;line-height:1.5;text-align:left;"> Never mind that Prop. 47 only loosens penalties for stealing under $950 — which a lot of the theft on TV was almost certainly above — or that the executive was killed by somebody he knew, not some random criminal. Right now, voters are concerned about drugs and crime, and lawmakers are responding to that. </p></div><div id="what-about-homelessness" style="padding-bottom:1px;padding-left:10px;padding-right:10px;padding-top:6px;"><h1 style="color:#000000;font-family:'Helvetica',Arial,sans-serif;font-size:32px;font-weight:Bold;line-height:1.75;margin:0;text-align:left;">What about homelessness?</h1></div><style>
  p span[style*="font-size"] { line-height: 1.6; }
</style><div style="padding-bottom:12px;padding-left:10px;padding-right:10px;padding-top:12px;"><p style="color:#000000;color:var(--wt-text-on-background-color) !important;font-family:'Helvetica',Arial,sans-serif;font-size:19px;line-height:1.5;text-align:left;"> As with crime, the rhetoric doesn’t always match the stats in the homelessness debate. For instance, it’s become something of a cliche to rag on San Francisco when talking about the state’s homelessness crisis, but the city doesn’t even have the largest unhoused population in the state. </p></div><style>
  p span[style*="font-size"] { line-height: 1.6; }
</style><div style="padding-bottom:12px;padding-left:10px;padding-right:10px;padding-top:12px;"><p style="color:#000000;color:var(--wt-text-on-background-color) !important;font-family:'Helvetica',Arial,sans-serif;font-size:19px;line-height:1.5;text-align:left;"> LA, San Jose, Oakland, Sacramento and San Diego beat it out in that order. In fact, San Francisco’s homeless population has <a class="link" href="https://sfstandard.com/2023/06/03/san-francisco-homelessness-count-data/?utm_source=morningtrojan.com&utm_medium=referral&utm_campaign=are-california-democrats-shifting-right" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer nofollow" style="-webkit-text-decoration:underline #ac2124;color:#ac2124;text-decoration:underline #ac2124;word-break:break-word;;">actually declined</a> in recent years. </p></div><style>
  p span[style*="font-size"] { line-height: 1.6; }
</style><div style="padding-bottom:12px;padding-left:10px;padding-right:10px;padding-top:12px;"><p style="color:#000000;color:var(--wt-text-on-background-color) !important;font-family:'Helvetica',Arial,sans-serif;font-size:19px;line-height:1.5;text-align:left;"> That’s in contrast to other California cities, where homelessness <a class="link" href="https://www.ppic.org/blog/homeless-populations-are-rising-around-california/?utm_source=morningtrojan.com&utm_medium=referral&utm_campaign=are-california-democrats-shifting-right" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer nofollow" style="-webkit-text-decoration:underline #ac2124;color:#ac2124;text-decoration:underline #ac2124;word-break:break-word;;">has gone up</a>. But most of that increase is because of people housed in shelters, not tents out on the street, meaning the increase hasn’t necessarily been visible. </p></div><style>
  p span[style*="font-size"] { line-height: 1.6; }
</style><div style="padding-bottom:12px;padding-left:10px;padding-right:10px;padding-top:12px;"><p style="color:#000000;color:var(--wt-text-on-background-color) !important;font-family:'Helvetica',Arial,sans-serif;font-size:19px;line-height:1.5;text-align:left;"> So Democrats’ recent willingness <a class="link" href="https://calmatters.org/housing/homelessness/2024/02/homeless-encampments-california-ban/?utm_source=morningtrojan.com&utm_medium=referral&utm_campaign=are-california-democrats-shifting-right" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer nofollow" style="-webkit-text-decoration:underline #ac2124;color:#ac2124;text-decoration:underline #ac2124;word-break:break-word;;">to ban encampments</a> near schools and parks — and entirely, when there’s shelter space available — might be more a reflection of voter fatigue than any particular spike. Lawmakers are frustrated by the seemingly intractable problem, and the easiest solution is to limit its most visible expression. </p></div><style>
  p span[style*="font-size"] { line-height: 1.6; }
</style><div style="padding-bottom:12px;padding-left:10px;padding-right:10px;padding-top:12px;"><p style="color:#000000;color:var(--wt-text-on-background-color) !important;font-family:'Helvetica',Arial,sans-serif;font-size:19px;line-height:1.5;text-align:left;"> “The general public has gone from considerable sympathy for people who are unhoused to significant anger about it,” Lockyer said. “It has kind of owned the present.” </p></div><div id="election-clarity" style="padding-bottom:1px;padding-left:10px;padding-right:10px;padding-top:6px;"><h1 style="color:#000000;font-family:'Helvetica',Arial,sans-serif;font-size:32px;font-weight:Bold;line-height:1.75;margin:0;text-align:left;">Election clarity</h1></div><style>

He’ll look to avoid the fate of a similarly progressive district attorney <a class="link" href="https://www.nytimes.com/2022/06/07/us/politics/chesa-boudin-recall-san-francisco.html?utm_source=morningtrojan.com&utm_medium=referral&utm_campaign=are-california-democrats-shifting-right" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer nofollow" style="-webkit-text-decoration:underline #ac2124;color:#ac2124;text-decoration:underline #ac2124;word-break:break-word;;">who was ousted</a> by San Francisco voters in 2022. </p></div><style>
