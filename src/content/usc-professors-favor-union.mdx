Their campaign is based heavily on speculation, rather than real information grounded in the empirical experience of thousands of unionized faculty across the U.S. who have already organized with UAW. None of this is a surprise. USC has opposed our right to form a union since the majority of us filed for an election in December 2024. Now, a small number of faculty are reiterating the Provost’s arguments. </p></div><style>

With a union, USC would, for the first time ever, have a legal obligation to negotiate with us over our pay, benefits and working conditions. </p></div><style>

By coming together across titles and disciplines, we have the power to make the improvements that we can’t make alone.
