</style><div style="padding-bottom:12px;padding-left:10px;padding-right:10px;padding-top:12px;"><p style="color:#000000;color:var(--wt-text-on-background-color) !important;font-family:'Helvetica',Arial,sans-serif;font-size:19px;line-height:1.5;text-align:left;"> Beong-Soo Kim, USC’s new president, has achieved a significant milestone that has largely been left unsaid: He is the first Asian American — in fact, the first person of color — to assume the school’s permanent presidency. </p></div><style>
  p span[style*="font-size"] { line-height: 1.6; }
</style><div style="padding-bottom:12px;padding-left:10px;padding-right:10px;padding-top:12px;"><p style="color:#000000;color:var(--wt-text-on-background-color) !important;font-family:'Helvetica',Arial,sans-serif;font-size:19px;line-height:1.5;text-align:left;"> You couldn’t be blamed for missing that fact. </p></div><style>
  p span[style*="font-size"] { line-height: 1.6; }
</style><div style="padding-bottom:12px;padding-left:10px;padding-right:10px;padding-top:12px;"><p style="color:#000000;color:var(--wt-text-on-background-color) !important;font-family:'Helvetica',Arial,sans-serif;font-size:19px;line-height:1.5;text-align:left;"> Since Kim’s appointment last week, the bevy of <a class="link" href="https://today.usc.edu/new-usc-president-beong-soo-kim/?utm_source=morningtrojan.com&utm_medium=referral&utm_campaign=a-historic-first-for-usc-downplayed" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer nofollow" style="-webkit-text-decoration:underline #ac2124;color:#ac2124;text-decoration:underline #ac2124;word-break:break-word;;">news releases</a>, <a class="link" href="https://www.instagram.com/reel/DUWX9c0CWNe/?utm_source=ig_web_copy_link&igsh=MzRlODBiNWFlZA==" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer nofollow" style="-webkit-text-decoration:underline #ac2124;color:#ac2124;text-decoration:underline #ac2124;word-break:break-word;;">social media videos</a>, and <a class="link" href="https://dailytrojan.com/2026/02/05/beong-soo-kim-delighted-usc-next-president/?utm_source=morningtrojan.com&utm_medium=referral&utm_campaign=a-historic-first-for-usc-downplayed" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer nofollow" style="-webkit-text-decoration:underline #ac2124;color:#ac2124;text-decoration:underline #ac2124;word-break:break-word;;">press interviews</a> pushed by USC have made no mention of the achievement. Accordingly, few <a class="link" href="https://www.nytimes.com/2026/02/04/us/usc-new-president-kim.html?utm_source=morningtrojan.com&utm_medium=referral&utm_campaign=a-historic-first-for-usc-downplayed" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer nofollow" style="-webkit-text-decoration:underline #ac2124;color:#ac2124;text-decoration:underline #ac2124;word-break:break-word;;">national</a> or <a class="link" href="https://www.latimes.com/california/story/2026-02-04/usc-president-beong-soo-kim?utm_source=morningtrojan.com&utm_medium=referral&utm_campaign=a-historic-first-for-usc-downplayed" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer nofollow" style="-webkit-text-decoration:underline #ac2124;color:#ac2124;text-decoration:underline #ac2124;word-break:break-word;;">local</a> media outlets have so much as hinted at it. </p></div><style>
  p span[style*="font-size"] { line-height: 1.6; }
</style><div style="padding-bottom:12px;padding-left:10px;padding-right:10px;padding-top:12px;"><p style="color:#000000;color:var(--wt-text-on-background-color) !important;font-family:'Helvetica',Arial,sans-serif;font-size:19px;line-height:1.5;text-align:left;"> It is a stark contrast to the fanfare when Wanda Austin, a Black woman, <a class="link" href="https://today.usc.edu/meet-wanda-austin-usc-interim-president/?utm_source=morningtrojan.com&utm_medium=referral&utm_campaign=a-historic-first-for-usc-downplayed" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer nofollow" style="-webkit-text-decoration:underline #ac2124;color:#ac2124;text-decoration:underline #ac2124;word-break:break-word;;">was installed</a> as interim president in 2018, and when Carol Folt <a class="link" href="https://www.cbsnews.com/news/carol-l-folt-university-of-southern-california-first-female-president/?utm_source=morningtrojan.com&utm_medium=referral&utm_campaign=a-historic-first-for-usc-downplayed" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer nofollow" style="-webkit-text-decoration:underline #ac2124;color:#ac2124;text-decoration:underline #ac2124;word-break:break-word;;">became the first woman</a> to serve as permanent USC president a year later. </p></div><style>
  p span[style*="font-size"] { line-height: 1.6; }
</style><div style="padding-bottom:12px;padding-left:10px;padding-right:10px;padding-top:12px;"><p style="color:#000000;color:var(--wt-text-on-background-color) !important;font-family:'Helvetica',Arial,sans-serif;font-size:19px;line-height:1.5;text-align:left;"> Kim has ascended to the presidency at a remarkably different time than Folt and Austin. An erratic second Trump administration has declared all-out war on what it has described as dangerously progressive universities, waging legal battles against schools that retain diversity, equity, and inclusion-coded language. </p></div><style>
  p span[style*="font-size"] { line-height: 1.6; }
</style><div style="padding-bottom:12px;padding-left:10px;padding-right:10px;padding-top:12px;"><p style="color:#000000;color:var(--wt-text-on-background-color) !important;font-family:'Helvetica',Arial,sans-serif;font-size:19px;line-height:1.5;text-align:left;"> In an interview Tuesday, Kim acknowledged that downplaying the historic nature of his presidency was a deliberate decision. But he denied that Trump-era anti-DEI attitudes played any role. </p></div><style>
  p span[style*="font-size"] { line-height: 1.6; }
</style><div style="padding-bottom:12px;padding-left:10px;padding-right:10px;padding-top:12px;"><p style="color:#000000;color:var(--wt-text-on-background-color) !important;font-family:'Helvetica',Arial,sans-serif;font-size:19px;line-height:1.5;text-align:left;"> “It was a personal decision that felt right for me,” Kim said. “It should be obvious to absolutely anyone, just from my name alone, that I am Asian American.” </p></div><style>
  p span[style*="font-size"] { line-height: 1.6; }
</style><div style="padding-bottom:12px;padding-left:10px;padding-right:10px;padding-top:12px;"><p style="color:#000000;color:var(--wt-text-on-background-color) !important;font-family:'Helvetica',Arial,sans-serif;font-size:19px;line-height:1.5;text-align:left;"> Eight decades ago, Kim’s ascension to the presidency would’ve been unthinkable. </p></div><style>
  p span[style*="font-size"] { line-height: 1.6; }
</style><div style="padding-bottom:12px;padding-left:10px;padding-right:10px;padding-top:12px;"><p style="color:#000000;color:var(--wt-text-on-background-color) !important;font-family:'Helvetica',Arial,sans-serif;font-size:19px;line-height:1.5;text-align:left;"> In World War II, when Japanese Americans were torn from their homes and locked in the barbed confines of California’s Owens Valley, scores of USC students were among them. </p></div><style>
  p span[style*="font-size"] { line-height: 1.6; }
</style><div style="padding-bottom:12px;padding-left:10px;padding-right:10px;padding-top:12px;"><p style="color:#000000;color:var(--wt-text-on-background-color) !important;font-family:'Helvetica',Arial,sans-serif;font-size:19px;line-height:1.5;text-align:left;"> When these students, known as Nisei, returned from their years-long internment, USC <a class="link" href="https://today.usc.edu/nisei-students-diplomas-usc/?utm_source=morningtrojan.com&utm_medium=referral&utm_campaign=a-historic-first-for-usc-downplayed" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer nofollow" style="-webkit-text-decoration:underline #ac2124;color:#ac2124;text-decoration:underline #ac2124;word-break:break-word;;">refused to release</a> their academic records, forcing many to restart their education elsewhere. </p></div><style>
  p span[style*="font-size"] { line-height: 1.6; }
</style><div style="padding-bottom:12px;padding-left:10px;padding-right:10px;padding-top:12px;"><p style="color:#000000;color:var(--wt-text-on-background-color) !important;font-family:'Helvetica',Arial,sans-serif;font-size:19px;line-height:1.5;text-align:left;"> Kim, of course, is Korean. His parents did not immigrate to the United States until the 1960s. It is hardly a surprise that a man of his background could ascend to a university presidency when Asian Americans have long played an outsized role in higher education. </p></div><style>
  p span[style*="font-size"] { line-height: 1.6; }
</style><div style="padding-bottom:12px;padding-left:10px;padding-right:10px;padding-top:12px;"><p style="color:#000000;color:var(--wt-text-on-background-color) !important;font-family:'Helvetica',Arial,sans-serif;font-size:19px;line-height:1.5;text-align:left;"> But it is undeniably significant that USC, which once assented to one of the great injustices of American history, is now led by an Asian man. </p></div><style>
  p span[style*="font-size"] { line-height: 1.6; }
</style><div style="padding-bottom:12px;padding-left:10px;padding-right:10px;padding-top:12px;"><p style="color:#000000;color:var(--wt-text-on-background-color) !important;font-family:'Helvetica',Arial,sans-serif;font-size:19px;line-height:1.5;text-align:left;"> In <a class="link" href="https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=BV75XPrv8Fc&utm_source=morningtrojan.com&utm_medium=referral&utm_campaign=a-historic-first-for-usc-downplayed" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer nofollow" style="-webkit-text-decoration:underline #ac2124;color:#ac2124;text-decoration:underline #ac2124;word-break:break-word;;">past interviews</a>, Kim, 53, has described his ethnic background as an “incredibly important part of my identity,” but has not appeared interested in discussing it at length — or espousing platitudes about diversity. </p></div><style>
  p span[style*="font-size"] { line-height: 1.6; }
</style><div style="padding-bottom:12px;padding-left:10px;padding-right:10px;padding-top:12px;"><p style="color:#000000;color:var(--wt-text-on-background-color) !important;font-family:'Helvetica',Arial,sans-serif;font-size:19px;line-height:1.5;text-align:left;"> Kim, the son of immigrants, who spent his childhood summers visiting Korea, was raised in Woodland Hills, whose population recorded in the <a class="link" href="https://www.laalmanac.com/population/po24la_1990.php?utm_source=morningtrojan.com&utm_medium=referral&utm_campaign=a-historic-first-for-usc-downplayed" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer nofollow" style="-webkit-text-decoration:underline #ac2124;color:#ac2124;text-decoration:underline #ac2124;word-break:break-word;;">1990 census</a> was less than 5% Asian. </p></div><style>
  p span[style*="font-size"] { line-height: 1.6; }
</style><div style="padding-bottom:12px;padding-left:10px;padding-right:10px;padding-top:12px;"><p style="color:#000000;color:var(--wt-text-on-background-color) !important;font-family:'Helvetica',Arial,sans-serif;font-size:19px;line-height:1.5;text-align:left;"> His attitude toward his ethnic identity appears not dissimilar from that of so many other second-generation Asian Americans, who are <a class="link" href="https://www.pewresearch.org/social-trends/2012/06/19/the-rise-of-asian-americans/?utm_source=morningtrojan.com&utm_medium=referral&utm_campaign=a-historic-first-for-usc-downplayed" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer nofollow" style="-webkit-text-decoration:underline #ac2124;color:#ac2124;text-decoration:underline #ac2124;word-break:break-word;;">popularly perceived</a> as assimilating quickly into the American mainstream. </p></div><style>
  p span[style*="font-size"] { line-height: 1.6; }
</style><div style="padding-bottom:12px;padding-left:10px;padding-right:10px;padding-top:12px;"><p style="color:#000000;color:var(--wt-text-on-background-color) !important;font-family:'Helvetica',Arial,sans-serif;font-size:19px;line-height:1.5;text-align:left;"> “I do recognize that this really matters to many members of the community,” Kim said. But, he added, “I would rather be judged ultimately based on what I end up doing” — and “not just that one part of my identity.” </p></div><style>
  p span[style*="font-size"] { line-height: 1.6; }
</style><div style="padding-bottom:12px;padding-left:10px;padding-right:10px;padding-top:12px;"><p style="color:#000000;color:var(--wt-text-on-background-color) !important;font-family:'Helvetica',Arial,sans-serif;font-size:19px;line-height:1.5;text-align:left;"><i>Tomo Chien can be reached at </i><i><a class="link" href="mailto:tomo@morningtrojan.com" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer nofollow" style="-webkit-text-decoration:underline #ac2124;color:#ac2124;text-decoration:underline #ac2124;word-break:break-word;;" data-skip-tracking="true">tomo@morningtrojan.com</a></i><i>.</i></p></div></div></div></div></div>
